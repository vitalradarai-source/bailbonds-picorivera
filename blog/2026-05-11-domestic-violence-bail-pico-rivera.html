The Charge: California Penal Code §273.5

PC §273.5 covers willful infliction of corporal injury on a spouse, cohabitant, fiancé/fiancée, or co-parent. It is a "wobbler" — chargeable as a felony or misdemeanor depending on injury severity, prior history, and prosecutorial discretion. LA County treats it as follows in 2026:

The 72-Hour Hold Myth

You may read online that California requires a "72-hour mandatory no-bail hold" on domestic violence arrests. It does not. The confusion stems from PC §136.2, which authorizes Criminal Protective Orders at arraignment — not a hold preventing release on bail. As soon as booking completes at LASD Pico Rivera Station, bail can be posted under the LA County schedule. A judge can later impose protective orders or conditions, but bail is available at booking.

The LASD Pico Rivera Station Booking Process

LASD Pico Rivera Station at 6631 Passons Blvd (562-699-1888) handles arrests within Pico Rivera city limits and surrounding LASD patrol areas (Whittier-adjacent, parts of Montebello, Commerce, East LA, and the unincorporated areas south of the 60). The booking process for a DV arrest follows the standard LASD pattern:

  1. Transport from arrest scene — typically 30-60 minutes. Most Pico Rivera DV arrests originate from 911 calls to a residence near Beverly Blvd, Whittier Blvd, or the apartment complexes off Slauson and Rosemead.
  2. LASD intake — 2-4 hours. Fingerprints, photographs, criminal history check, charge entry. Defendant cannot bail out until intake completes.
  3. Bond posting — 30-60 minutes. The cosigner signs the indemnity agreement; the bondsman files the surety bond with LASD records. Premium is 10% of the bail amount, paid as a lump sum or no-interest installments.
  4. Release processing — 1-2 hours. Jail verifies the bond, checks for outstanding holds, returns property, walks defendant out.

If LASD Pico Rivera Station is at capacity or the case requires longer holding, the defendant transfers to Inmate Reception Center (450 Bauchet St, LA) or Twin Towers Correctional Facility (441 Bauchet St). Transfer adds 12-24 hours and resets the booking timeline.

What Happens at Arraignment (Whittier Courthouse)

After release on bail, the defendant must appear at arraignment — typically Whittier Courthouse (7339 Painter Ave, Whittier 90602), usually within 48 hours of arrest, not counting weekends or holidays. At arraignment:

  • The defendant enters a plea (most often "not guilty" with counsel present).
  • The judge can issue a Criminal Protective Order under PC §136.2 — a separate order that may restrict contact with the alleged victim, regardless of bail status.
  • Bail conditions can be modified. Common conditions: surrender firearms, complete a 52-week DV treatment program, no-contact order, residence away from the protected party.
  • Future court dates are scheduled. Missing any date triggers bond forfeiture and a bench warrant.

Premium Installments — How the $5,000 Gets Paid

Most Pico Rivera families do not pay the $5,000 premium in one lump sum. A no-interest installment plan splits the 10% across weeks or months. Department of Insurance rules:

  • No interest. Total stays at exactly 10% of the bail amount. CDI prohibits interest on the bond premium itself.
  • No origination fee, no application fee. The bondsman charges the premium and nothing else.
  • Income-based qualification. Stable employment and ties to Pico Rivera, Whittier, Montebello, Downey, Commerce, East LA, or Norwalk count more than a credit score.
  • Bond posts the same day. Release doesn't wait for the full premium to be paid.
