The LASD Pico Rivera Station is located at 6631 Passons Blvd, Pico Rivera, CA 90660. The Booking Process at Pico Rivera Sheriff's Station

Booking at any LASD station follows a standardized process administered by sheriff's custody staff. Here is what happens after an arrest in Pico Rivera:

Step 1
Arrival & Intake
The arresting deputy transports the individual to Pico Rivera Station. The arrest report is logged, the charges are recorded, and the booking number is assigned. This initial intake is when the clock starts on the 48-hour arraignment window under California Penal Code § 825.
Step 2
Property Inventory
All personal property — phone, wallet, keys, jewelry — is catalogued and secured. The individual receives a written receipt. From this point, direct communication with the outside becomes limited to the jail's internal phone system.
Step 3
Live Scan Fingerprinting
Electronic fingerprints are taken and run against state and federal databases. This step identifies prior criminal history, checks for outstanding warrants from other jurisdictions, and confirms the person's identity. An active warrant from another county can result in a hold that complicates immediate release.
Step 4
Photograph (Mugshot)
Booking photos are taken for the official record. Once the booking is processed, these become part of the LASD public record.
Step 5
Medical Screening
California law requires a health screening for every person booked into jail. Staff assess medications, injury, sobriety, and mental health needs. This step can take longer for individuals with medical conditions that require evaluation.
Step 6
Bail Determination
Bail is set according to the Los Angeles County Felony and Misdemeanor Bail Schedules, published annually by the Superior Court. For most charges, this is automatic and does not require a hearing. A judge may modify the amount at arraignment.
Step 7
Housing Assignment or Transfer
The individual is assigned to a cell within the station or flagged for transfer to a county facility. Felony defendants, those needing medical housing, or those with active holds are most likely to be transferred to Men's Central Jail or the Century Regional Detention Facility in Lynwood.

Total booking time at Pico Rivera Station is typically 2 to 8 hours for misdemeanors and 6 to 16 hours for felonies. If the individual is transferred to a county facility, add another several hours for transport and re-processing.

The LA County Bail Schedule — 2026 Amounts

Los Angeles County uses a standardized bail schedule published annually by the Superior Court. The schedule sets the default bail amount for each charge at the time of booking — before any judge reviews the case. Here are representative amounts from the current schedule:

Charge Penal Code Bail Amount
Simple assault PC 240 $5,000
Battery on a person PC 242 $5,000
Assault with a deadly weapon PC 245(a)(1) $30,000
Assault with a firearm PC 245(a)(2) $50,000
Robbery (second degree) PC 211 $50,000
Robbery (first degree) PC 211 $100,000
Residential burglary PC 459 $50,000
DUI (no injury) VC 23152 $5,000
DUI with injury VC 23153 $100,000
Possession for sale (HS 11351) HS 11351 $30,000

These amounts apply at the time of booking. At arraignment — which must occur within 48 hours of arrest under PC § 825 (excluding Sundays and court holidays) — a judge may increase, decrease, or eliminate bail entirely based on flight risk, criminal history, and community ties.

Why post bail before arraignment? If you post bail before the arraignment, the defendant is released at the scheduled amount. If the case goes to arraignment with bail unposted, a judge may raise the amount — particularly for serious felonies or defendants with prior records. Acting quickly locks in the lower schedule amount.

How Bail Bonds Work in California

A bail bond is a contract between you, the bail bondsman, and the court. When a licensed bail agent posts a bond, they guarantee the court that the defendant will appear at all required hearings. In exchange, you pay the bondsman a premium — a percentage of the total bail amount.

Under California Insurance Code § 1800.4, the standard bail bond premium is 10% of the total bail amount. This premium is set by state law and is non-negotiable at the standard rate. For a $30,000 bail amount, your out-of-pocket cost is $3,000 — not $30,000.

Key things to understand about bail bonds in California:

  • The premium is non-refundable. Whether the case takes six weeks or two years, the 10% fee is earned when the bond is posted. It is not a deposit — it is payment for the service of posting the bond.
  • Collateral may be required. For high-bail amounts, the bondsman may require a co-signer and collateral (real estate, vehicle title) to secure the bond. We discuss this clearly before moving forward.
  • Payment plans are available. We understand that most families don't have $3,000 to $10,000 in liquid cash at 2am. We offer flexible payment arrangements for qualifying clients.
  • You can start the process by phone. The entire bail bond agreement can be completed remotely — by phone, email, or electronic signature. You do not need to come to an office or meet us at the jail.

Common Complications That Delay Release from Pico Rivera

Most bookings proceed straightforwardly — but some situations complicate or delay release. Here are the most common ones we handle:

  • Transfer to Men's Central Jail. Defendants with serious felony charges are routinely transferred from Pico Rivera Station to Men's Central Jail downtown. If this happens, we re-route the bond to the new facility — no extra cost to you.
  • Outstanding warrants from other jurisdictions. A warrant from a neighboring county — say, Orange County or San Bernardino — can result in a hold that prevents release even after bail is posted in LA County. We identify these immediately and advise you on options.
  • Court holiday or weekend timing. If the 48-hour arraignment window falls on a Sunday or court holiday, it extends to the next court day. Getting bail posted before this window closes prevents a judge from raising the amount.
  • Arraignment timing. We always prioritize posting bail before the arraignment hearing to lock in the scheduled bail amount. This is especially critical in cases with serious charges.
