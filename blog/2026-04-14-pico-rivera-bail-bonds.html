Where Pico Rivera Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Pico Rivera is transported to the LA County Sheriff's Pico Rivera Station at 6631 Passons Boulevard, Pico Rivera, CA 90660 (phone: 562-949-2421). Pico Rivera contracts with the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department for police services. Arrests are handled out of the LASD Pico Rivera Station at 6631 Passons Boulevard, with most felony custody moved to Men's Central Jail or CRDF (Century Regional Detention Facility) in Lynwood.

Pico Rivera in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Pico Rivera is a Los Angeles County contract city — no Pico Rivera PD. The Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department operates out of the Pico Rivera Sheriff's Station at 6631 Passons Boulevard. Deputies book directly into the LASD jail system, with most inmates routed through LA County IRC (Inmate Reception Center) at 441 Bauchet Street in downtown Los Angeles. This is a much longer transport than an OC arrest and adds processing time to any release.

The city sits at the junction of the 605 freeway and the San Gabriel River, bounded by Whittier to the south and Montebello to the west. Pico Rivera's commercial spine runs along Whittier Boulevard and Rosemead Boulevard, with heavy retail at Pico Rivera Towne Center and the Slauson Avenue corridor. This produces a specific set of call patterns: DUI stops along the 605, gang-related arrests that LASD investigates aggressively, and retail theft from the Whittier Boulevard strip.

One practical reality of LASD bookings: the IRC in downtown LA is notoriously slow — release times of 8 to 24 hours post-bond are routine, sometimes longer on weekends. This is a system capacity issue, not a bondsman issue. The fastest path in a Pico Rivera case is posting the bond before the defendant is transported from the Pico Rivera Sheriff's Station, if possible. Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Pico Rivera arrest actually get processed?

Pico Rivera is a LASD contract city. Arrests book out of the Pico Rivera Sheriff's Station at 6631 Passons Boulevard, with transport to LA County IRC at 441 Bauchet Street downtown. IRC release times run 8–24 hours post-bond on weekends.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Pico Rivera?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Pico Rivera bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Los Angeles County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Pico Rivera DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
